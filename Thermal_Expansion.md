# Thermal Expansion

## Aim of the Experiment
To determine the coefficient of linear thermal expansion of three different metals by measuring the change in their length with temperature using Pullinger's apparatus.

## Idea behind the Experiment
When the temperature of a solid changes, the average separation between its atoms also changes due to variations in the amplitude of atomic vibrations. As temperature increases, atoms vibrate more vigorously about their equilibrium positions. Because the interatomic potential is slightly asymmetric, the mean separation between atoms increases, resulting in an expansion of the material. Conversely, cooling reduces the amplitude of vibration and the material contracts.

For most solids over a moderate temperature range (particularly near room temperature), the change in length is approximately proportional to both the original length of the specimen and the change in temperature. This behaviour is described by the linear coefficient of thermal expansion.

The relationship between the change in length and temperature is given by:

$$\Delta L = L_0 \alpha (T - T_0)$$

where
- $Δ𝐿 =$ change in length of the rod
- $𝐿_0 =$ original length at temperature 
- $T-T_0 =$ change in temperature
- $\alpha =$ coefficient of linear thermal expansion of the material

The coefficient $\alpha$ represents the fractional change in length per unit temperature change. It depends on the material and has units of $\degree \text{C}^{-1} \ \text{or} \ \text{K}^{-1}$. Thus, a plot of the spherometer reading, $\Delta L$ versus temperature, $T$ should produce a straight line. The slope of this line $=L_0 \alpha$

Hence the coefficient of linear thermal expansion can be determined as

$$\alpha=\frac{\text{slope}}{L_0}$$

```python
# Thermal Expansion for brass
ref_temp= 24.1 #°C
temp=np.array([80, 78, 76, 74, 72, 70, 68, 66, 64, 62, 60, 58, 56, 54, 52, 50, 48, 46, 44, 42, 40, 38, 36, 34, 32, 30])-24.1
length1=np.array([.75, .63, .6, .56, .53, .49, .45, .42, .38, .36, .34, .31, .29, .27, .24, .22, .20, .18, .15, .13, .11, .1, .08, .06, .04, .02])
length2=np.array([.67, .62, .58, .54, .51, .48, .45, .42, .39, .36, .33, .30, .27, .25, .22, .20, .18, .16, .14, .11, .09, .08, .05, .03, .02, .0])
length3=np.array([.65, .61, .57, .54, .51, .48, .45, .42, .39, .36, .33, .30, .28, .25, .23, .21, .19, .16, .14, .12, .1, .08, .06, .04, .02, .01])
length= (length1+length2+length3)/3
std_err= np.std([length1,length2,length3], axis=0, ddof=1)/np.sqrt(3)


#plotting length vs temperature with error bars and fitting a line
slope, intercept, r_value, p_value, std_err_fit = stats.linregress(temp, length)
print(f"Slope: {slope} cm/°C")
print(f"Intercept: {intercept} cm")
eq_label=rf"Fit: y={slope:.4f}*x + {intercept:.4f}"
plt.errorbar(temp, length, yerr=std_err, fmt='o--', color='blue', label='Average Length with Std Error')
plt.plot(temp, intercept + slope * temp, 'r', label=eq_label)
plt.xlabel('Temperature (°C)')
plt.ylabel('Length (cm)')
plt.title('Length vs Temperature with Error Bars')
plt.legend()
plt.grid()
plt.show()

#r2 value for the fit
ss_res = np.sum((length - (intercept + slope * temp))**2)
ss_tot = np.sum((length - np.mean(length))**2)
r_squared = 1 - (ss_res / ss_tot)
print(f"R-squared: {r_squared}")

# calculating the coefficient of linear expansion
l_0= 708  #mm Initial length at reference temperature
alpha = slope / l_0
print(f"Coefficient of linear expansion (alpha): {alpha} 1/°C")
```
